11. Conclusion

The analyzed binary is a sophisticated, unencrypted Linux ELF malware with strong networking and cryptographic capabilities. It establishes a persistent daemon, communicates via a custom protocol, and utilizes complex crypto (RSA, TEA, MD5, PEM parsing) likely to secure its C2 traffic or decrypt payloads. The presence of strings referencing /dev/urandom, encrypted PEM headers, and network commands points to a Remote Access Trojan (RAT) or Botnet agent designed for stealthy, encrypted remote control. Priority should be given to identifying the source of the infection and blocking its network communication.
